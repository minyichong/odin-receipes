Easy Zucchini Noodles with Homemade Chili Oil

Zucchini was my choice of vegetable at the time because summer zucchini and squash is currently in season. YOu can switch/add vegetables as you like such as carrots, kale, asparagus, brocolli or cabbage,etc.

For noodles, I used somen, which is also called wheat noodles. If you want to make it gluten-free, you can use rice noodles. It will be as delicious!

Ingredients

  • 3 servings of dried somen (wheat noodles)/ any thin-medium noodles
  • 1 zucchini, half-moon sliced
  • 1/2 onion
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp agave nectar/maple syrup/ raw sugar
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 to 3tbsp homemade chili oil *optional or storebought Lao Gan Ma (a chili oil brand)

    • If you like to make some homemade chili oil. refer below for the recipe! You won't regret it !

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil, cook somen by following directions of package youâ€™re using. When somen has 1 minute to finish, add zucchini & onion into pot and boil it together.
  2. Drain and rinse under cold water. Drain completely and place in a large mixing bowl. Add soy sauce, nectar, and chili oil then mix well. Garnish with sesame seeds and enjoy!

* Here is the recipe of homemade chili oil if any of you are like me that loves making things from scratch !

Homemade Chili Oil

Ingredients for chili oil

  • 1 cup vegetable/avocado/canola/peanut oil- in separate bowl
  • 2 large shallots, chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp chinese chili bean sauce, toban jiang
  • 1/2 cup red pepper flaskes for mild version or Thai chili flakes for spicer version
  • 1 1/2 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • small pinch of Chinese five spice powder, *optional
  • 1 tbsp sichuan peppercorns, *optional
  • 1 tsp msg, *optional
  1. Toast sichuan pepper corn on a dry wok or pan for 45 seconds to 1 minute over medium heat. Grind with mortar and pestle or coffee/spice grinder to fine but still a little coarse powder. Set aside.
  2. Heat a wok over medium low to medium low heat, add 1/4 cup of oil and chopped shallot. Stir fry the shallot for 4 to 5 minutes or until they are golden brown.
  3. Add soy sauce around edge of the wok, so it will give nice smokey flavor. Also add toban djan at this point. Stir fry the sauce and shallot mixture for 1 to 2 minutes.
  4. Add remaining oil, red pepper flakes, sugar, salt, Chinese five spices, msg and sichuan pepper corn. Stir and cook for additional 1 to 2 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool then store in an air tight container. Keep it in a room temperature is fine and it will last 15 days. Enjoy!
